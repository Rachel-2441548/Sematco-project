3.Laboratory Department

Department Overview:
The Laboratory Department is primarily responsible for receiving building material samples from construction sites or soil samples from the Drilling Department and conducting relevant physical and chemical tests in accordance with international standards. On-site testing is also carried out as required, including both destructive and non-destructive methods. All test results are submitted to the Technical Department for report generation.

The department is divided into three units: the Soil Lab, Concrete & Steel Lab, and Chemical Lab. Each unit is managed by experienced personnel and equipped with modern, regularly calibrated testing equipment.

Scope of Work:

  1. Determine soil physical and strength parameters including moisture content, specific gravity, grain size distribution (sieve and hydrometer analysis), classification, Atterberg limits, soil density, permeability, direct shear, triaxial, and consolidation tests.
  2. Conduct in-situ plate load tests to evaluate soil bearing capacity.
  3. Measure soil field density using sand cone testing.
  4. Analyze soil and water chemical properties such as sulfate content, chloride content, pH levels, and heavy metals.
  5. Determine soil resistivity for corrosion analysis.
  6. Test aggregate properties, including L.A. abrasion, sand equivalent, and chemical composition.
  7. Analyze physical and chemical properties of cement.
  8. Take fresh concrete cubes for compressive strength testing.
  9. Evaluate strength of existing concrete structures using non-destructive methods (ultrasonic, Schmidt hammer) and destructive methods (core extraction and crushing).
  10. Perform load testing on existing structures and monitor deflection of structural elements.
  11. Conduct sampling and testing of asphalt mixes.
  12. Test tensile strength of steel reinforcement bars.
